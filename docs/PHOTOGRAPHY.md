# Photography Direction — Ahamed Web Studio

`DESIGN_SYSTEM.md` §5 establishes the principles (exceptional, real, no stock, full-width). This document makes those principles concrete enough to actually brief or shoot against.

## 1. What gets photographed

Two distinct categories, each with its own treatment:

- **Case-study/work imagery** — screenshots and captures of the actual websites we build. This is the majority of the site's imagery and does the heaviest trust-building work (`TRUST.md`, `CASE_STUDY_STRATEGY.md`).
- **Studio/founder imagery** — photography of Najman and/or the studio, used sparingly (primarily `/studio`, possibly a small presence on `/partners`). This is about making "real founder" (`TRUST.md` §2) tangible, not about building out a lifestyle photo library.

## 2. Studio/founder photography direction

- **Portrait, not lifestyle-stock.** A real, well-composed portrait of Najman — not a generic "person typing on laptop in a bright office" stock-style shot. Should feel personal and specific, not interchangeable with any agency's About page.
- **No staged "office culture" photography** (team high-fiving, whiteboard brainstorming, coffee-cup flat lays) — these are generic agency clichés that read as filler, and they're not honest if the studio is a small/solo-led operation (`PRD.md` §2 explicitly resists over-claiming scale).
- **Environment, if used at all, should be true** — an actual workspace, not a rented/staged set. Authenticity matters more than polish here; a slightly imperfect real photo beats a slick fake one.
- **Flat-lay product/device photography** (e.g. a laptop showing a case-study site on a clean desk) is acceptable *only* if it meets the same "real, not staged-stock" bar — and should be rare, not a recurring device.

## 3. Treatment & consistency

- **Warm, not neutral or cold.** All photography — studio and case-study alike — should be color-graded or selected to sit comfortably against the cream/navy/orange palette (`DESIGN_SYSTEM.md` §2). A cool, blue-white studio portrait next to warm case-study screenshots will look like two different websites stitched together.
- **Consistent editing treatment across every photo on the site** — same warmth, same contrast/exposure sensibility, same restraint (no heavy filters, no dramatic vignettes). One person should be able to look at any photo on the site and recognize it belongs to this brand, independent of subject matter.
- **Brightness:** favor natural, well-lit, slightly warm-bright over moody/dark — moody/dark contrast lighting reads as trying too hard for "premium," which is exactly the over-designed impression `DESIGN_SYSTEM.md` §1 warns against ("less, but better").

## 4. Case-study imagery specifics

(Extends `DESIGN_SYSTEM.md` §5 and `CASE_STUDY_STRATEGY.md` §4 — see those for the presentation rules; this section is about the photography/capture treatment itself.)

- Capture real, deployed (or near-final) screens at high resolution — never a low-fidelity design-tool export standing in for a real screenshot.
- Responsive views should be captured as real browser/device viewports at actual breakpoint widths, not simulated or illustrated.
- If a project involved real-world/physical elements (e.g. a physical space for a hospitality client), on-location photography is welcome and should follow the same warm, real, non-stock treatment as everything else — but only if it's genuine project photography, not generic stock standing in for "atmosphere."

## 5. What to avoid entirely

- Stock photography of any kind, anywhere on the site (`DESIGN_SYSTEM.md` §5)
- Generic "team collaborating" or "office culture" imagery
- Cold/blue color grading that clashes with the warm palette
- Heavy filters, dramatic vignettes, or over-processed "cinematic" treatments
- Device mockups or browser-chrome frames standing in for real screenshots (`DESIGN_SYSTEM.md` §5)

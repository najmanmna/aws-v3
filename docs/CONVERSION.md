# Conversion Strategy — Ahamed Web Studio

Conversion here means a qualified inquiry (`PRD.md` §7) — not raw traffic, not time-on-site for its own sake. This document defines how the site is structured to move a well-fit visitor toward reaching out, calmly, without ever tipping into the hard-sell tactics the brand explicitly rejects (`COPY_GUIDELINES.md` §2, `TRUST.md` §5).

## 1. Primary and secondary CTA

- **Primary CTA: start a conversation about a project** — language along the lines of "Start a project" / "Talk to us about your project" (`COPY_GUIDELINES.md` §6). This is the one action the entire site is built to drive.
- **Secondary CTA: view the work** ("See our work" / a direct link into `/work`). For a visitor not ready to reach out, this is the productive next step — it deepens trust rather than losing them.
- There is no tertiary CTA. Newsletter signups, resource downloads, "book a free audit" — none of these fit a boutique-selectivity positioning (`POSITIONING.md` §4) and all dilute focus on the one action that matters.

## 2. Where CTAs appear

- **Home:** primary CTA appears once in the hero region (or immediately after it) and once more at the end of the page — never more than twice. Repeating it every section reads as pressure, which contradicts the calm, understated brand voice.
- **Every case study ends with a CTA** — after a visitor finishes a case study, they're at their highest point of conviction on that page; don't let that momentum dead-end. Offer both the primary CTA and a link to another case study (`CASE_STUDY_STRATEGY.md` §6's "Reflection" beat is a natural transition point).
- **`/work` (the index)** should link to `/contact` at the end, but doesn't need a CTA between every entry — let the work itself do the work; don't interrupt the portfolio's rhythm with repeated sales prompts.
- **`/partners`** can use slightly more direct CTA language than Home, since agencies are evaluating a professional partnership, not being sold to casually (`TRUST.md` §4) — e.g. "Talk to us about a project" rather than a softer founder-facing phrasing.
- **`/contact`** is the destination, not a place that needs its own CTA beyond the form itself.

## 3. Work → Contact flow

Yes — `/work` and every individual case study should link toward `/contact`, directly or by way of another case study first. The site should never let a convinced visitor hit a dead end and have to hunt for how to reach out.

## 4. Clicks to enquiry

**Target: two clicks, from anywhere on the site, to a contact/enquiry action.** (e.g. Home → Contact, or Case Study → Contact.) This isn't a minimalism exercise for its own sake — it's a direct expression of `TRUST.md`'s point that a contact form should feel like the natural next step, not a funnel with friction deliberately inserted. If a redesign of the IA ever pushes this past two clicks, that's a regression worth catching (see `SITE_STRUCTURE.md` §4 nav requirements).

## 5. Visual-to-copy ratio

**Roughly 70% visual, 30% text**, especially on Home and within case studies. People scan; they don't read paragraphs of agency self-description (`COPY_GUIDELINES.md` §3). This ratio is a direct expression of `PRD.md` §6 ("spend more time showing our work than talking about ourselves") made measurable:

- Favor a strong image plus one confident line of copy over three sentences of explanation.
- If a section is more than roughly 30% text, look for what can be cut or replaced with something the visitor can *see* instead of read.
- This ratio applies less strictly to `/studio` and long-form case-study narrative sections (`CASE_STUDY_STRATEGY.md` §6), where a visitor has already opted into reading — but even there, err toward brevity.

## 6. What conversion here explicitly does not mean

- It does not mean maximizing form submissions at any cost — a form that's easy for *anyone* to fill out, including poor-fit price-shoppers, is working against `PRD.md` §5's filtering goal.
- It does not mean urgency/scarcity tactics, countdowns, exit-intent popups, or chat-widget pressure — all incompatible with the calm brand voice (`COPY_GUIDELINES.md` §2) and the trust model in `TRUST.md`.
- Success is a *qualified* inquiry, not a maximized one (`PRD.md` §7) — if a conversion tactic would increase volume but dilute fit, it's the wrong call for this brand.
